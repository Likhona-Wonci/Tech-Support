Troubleshooting Steps

Follow these steps to troubleshoot common issues:

  1. Step 1: Compile Data
    Gather details about the problem, including symptoms and conditions for replication.
  2. Step 2: Explain the Issue
    Describe the problem thoroughly, asking questions such as:
    • What signs and symptoms are present?
    • When does the issue start to happen?
    • Where is the issue happening?
    • What circumstances exist when the issue arises?
    • Can the issue be replicated?
  3. Step 3: Ascertain the Most Likely Cause
    Use trial and error to identify possible causes, starting with the most obvious. Consider split-half troubleshooting for complex systems.
  4. Step 4: Develop a Strategy and Test a Fix
    Create and test a solution. If unsuccessful, return to Step 3.
  5. Step 5: Put the Solution into Practice
    Implement the fix and confirm it resolves the issue.
  6. Step 6: Examine the Outcomes
    Monitor for additional issues post-fix.
  7. Step 7: Record the Procedure
    Document actions taken and solutions, including unsuccessful attempts.
